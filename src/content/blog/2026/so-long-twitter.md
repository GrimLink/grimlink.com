I joined Twitter on February 6, 2009. I know because my very first tweet was: _"started to use twitter"_ — a sentence so earnest it could only have been written by a 18-year-old excited about a new thing on the internet.

Sixteen years later, I exported my archive and closed the door.

2011 and 2012 were the peak — nearly half my entire tweet history in just two years. I was going to Joomla events, actively gaming, and genuinely excited about everything I was doing. Twitter was where I shared all of it in real time.

After 2014 the numbers drop sharply, and they never really recovered. The platform changed. I changed. The ratio of signal to noise kept shifting in the wrong direction.

The 2022 acquisition made it worse in ways that were hard to ignore. The toxicity crept up, the moderation dissolved, and the people who had made it worth visiting started leaving. I stuck around longer than I should have — out of habit more than anything — but when Mastodon and Bluesky became real alternatives, the decision made itself.

## What made it here

Going through the archive was a genuine trip down memory lane. I decided to pull the highlights into this blog as a proper archive — the Joomla events, the gaming milestones, the personal moments that were worth keeping.

The bulk of it came from the middle years — 2013 through 2022 — when I was posting most consistently. But the early years (2009–2012) had a different quality to them: Joomla Days Netherlands, Joomla at the Barn, getting a PS Vita on launch day, finishing Gravity Rush, Zwarte Cross with Kamelot playing — all of it captured in real time, a little rough around the edges in wording, but real.

Most of it lived and died as ephemeral posts on a platform that no longer exists in any meaningful form. Some of it is now here instead, where I control it.

## Good times

For all its flaws, early Twitter was genuinely fun. A small timeline of people you actually knew, sharing things they were actually doing. The platform that exists today barely resembles that.

I don't miss it. But I'm glad the memories are somewhere they won't disappear.
